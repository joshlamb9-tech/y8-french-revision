Knowledge Checklist

Track what you know for CE Level 2

Home Grammar Reference Knowledge Checklist

How to use this checklist: Go through each item and tick off what you feel confident with. Items you can't tick need more practice!

Vocabulary Checklist

1. Personal Information

  • I can introduce myself (name, age, birthday)
  • I can describe my family members
  • I can describe my personality
  • I can describe my physical appearance

2. School

  • I know the names of school subjects
  • I can describe my timetable and school day
  • I can give opinions about school subjects
  • I can describe school facilities

3. Home and Local Area

  • I can describe my house
  • I know the names of rooms and furniture
  • I can describe my town and local facilities
  • I can give and understand directions

4. Free Time and Hobbies

  • I know vocabulary for sports and activities
  • I can describe my hobbies and pastimes
  • I can give opinions about activities

5. Daily Routine

  • I can tell the time in French
  • I know time expressions (le matin, le soir, etc.)
  • I can use reflexive verbs to describe my routine
  • I can describe a typical day

6. Food and Drink

  • I know vocabulary for meals and food items
  • I can express food preferences
  • I can order food in a restaurant or café
  • I can use partitive articles with food

7. Health

  • I know the names of body parts
  • I can describe common illnesses and symptoms
  • I can explain what's wrong at the doctor's
  • I can understand medical advice

8. Weather and Seasons

  • I know the names of seasons
  • I can describe the weather
  • I can talk about activities in different weather/seasons

9. Holidays and Travel

  • I know the names of countries
  • I know types of transport
  • I can describe holiday activities
  • I can describe a past holiday in the perfect tense

10. Future Plans

  • I can express intentions using aller + infinitive
  • I can talk about future activities
  • I can describe my aspirations

Grammar Checklist

Present Tense

  • I can conjugate regular -ER verbs (parler, jouer, regarder)
  • I can conjugate regular -IR verbs (finir, choisir)
  • I can conjugate regular -RE verbs (vendre, attendre)
  • I know the present tense of avoir (to have)
  • I know the present tense of être (to be)
  • I know the present tense of aller (to go)
  • I know the present tense of faire (to do/make)
  • I can use reflexive verbs (se lever, se coucher)

Perfect Tense (Passé Composé)

  • I understand when to use the perfect tense
  • I can form the perfect tense with avoir
  • I can form the perfect tense with être
  • I know which verbs use être (MRS VAN DER TRAMP)
  • I can make the past participle agree with être verbs
  • I know common irregular past participles (fait, vu, pris, etc.)
  • I can form negative sentences in the perfect tense

Articles and Nouns

  • I can use definite articles (le, la, l', les)
  • I can use indefinite articles (un, une, des)
  • I can use partitive articles (du, de la, de l', des)
  • I know that articles change after negatives (de/d')
  • I can identify masculine and feminine nouns

Adjectives

  • I can make adjectives agree (masculine/feminine, singular/plural)
  • I know that most adjectives come AFTER the noun
  • I know BAGS adjectives come BEFORE the noun
  • I can use common adjectives correctly (petit, grand, beau, bon)

Negatives

  • I can form negative sentences with ne...pas
  • I can use ne...jamais (never)
  • I can use ne...plus (no longer)
  • I can form negatives in the perfect tense

Prepositions

  • I know common prepositions (à, de, dans, sur, avec, sans)
  • I can use à + le = au and à + les = aux
  • I can use de + le = du and de + les = des

Skills Checklist

Listening

  • I can understand simple conversations
  • I can pick out key information from spoken French
  • I can understand past tense descriptions
  • I can understand opinions and reasons

Speaking

  • I can introduce myself and give personal information
  • I can have simple conversations on familiar topics
  • I can describe past events
  • I can give opinions with reasons
  • I can ask and answer questions

Reading

  • I can understand simple texts on familiar topics
  • I can pick out key information from written texts
  • I can understand past tense narratives
  • I can understand opinions in written form

Writing

  • I can write about myself and my life
  • I can describe past events in writing
  • I can give opinions with reasons
  • I can write accurately with correct spelling
  • I can use a range of vocabulary and grammar

What's Next?

Once you've gone through this checklist:

  • Green areas (confident): Keep practicing to maintain your knowledge
  • Amber areas (unsure): Review the topic pages and practice with sentence builders
  • Red areas (not confident): Prioritize these topics in your revision
